## Project Overview
This project analyzes a global survey of data professionals to understand trends, preferences, and challenges across different countries and job roles. The goal is to uncover insights into salaries, skills, career entry difficulty, and overall job satisfaction.

Key questions explored include:
> Which data roles earn the highest average salaries
> Where data professionals are geographically located
> Which programming languages are most popular in the data field
> How difficult it is to break into data-related careers
> How satisfied professionals are with their salary and work-life balance

## Skills & Tools Used
> Power BI
> Excel
> Data cleaning and preparation
> Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
> Grouping and aggregation
> Data visualization and dashboard design
> Interpreting and communicating insights
> Business and career-focused data storytelling

## Key Insights
> Data Scientists and Data Engineers earn the highest average salaries among data roles.
> North America and Europe host the largest concentration of data professionals.
> Python and SQL dominate as the most popular programming languages in the data field.
> A significant portion of respondents find it challenging to enter data-related careers.
> Higher salary satisfaction is strongly associated with better work-life balance.
> Career happiness varies by role, with specialized and senior roles reporting higher satisfaction.
